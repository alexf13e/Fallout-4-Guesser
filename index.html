Fallout 4 Guesser


Figure out where an image was taken in the world of Fallout 4 and get a score based on how close you are.
There are currently 788 locations accross the Commonwealth, 3 gamemodes along with a custom mode where you can set various options and get a code for others to play with the same rules and locations.

Idea taken from these games, mainly gtaguessr for showing me the concept is actually fun and worth pursuing for other games:
https://www.geoguessr.com
https://gtaguessr.com

You may also be interested in https://lostgamer.io. They've made geoguessr games in other game maps including Skyrim (plus they have, like, actual servers and stuff). I'm not affiliated with them at all, I just played the Elden Ring one and thought it was interesting to see how full 3D streetview-esque locations work vs just images.


Lastly, I have made a site for playing bingo games with objectives in Fallout 4, try it here


Controls

Scroll to zoom, click and drag to pan - works for the map and game image.
Click on the map to place your guess.
Shortcuts: Space to confirm a guess and move to the next round, shift to show/hide the Pip-Boy


Known Bugs

  • Some world changes that take place in the game are shown on the map but not in the game images, as they were taken on a fresh save. These include the Prydwen being over the airport, and the USS Constitution being on top of the skyscraper (it is also shown as being on the bank where it starts).
  • Some areas of water on the satellite map have visible borders between differently coloured regions. Not much I can do, this is just how they are in the actual game and the map rendering program renders them as they are.
  • Not really a bug but the pipboy style map (i.e. the non-satellite one) is quite inaccurate in some places. Also sometimes it flies to the side while zooming.

If you see something wrong with the game, there is a button to click which will copy useful information. Just send that to me with a description of what the issue seems to be, and maybe I'll even try and fix it.
